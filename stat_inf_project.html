Part 1: Data

The GSS -General Social Survey- is a socilogical survey, monitoring the societal change and contempary american society. it aims to gather data to explain trends and constants in attitudes, behaviors and attributes; to examine the strucure and functionaioning of society in general as well as the role played by relevant subgroups.

The dataset used for this project is an extract of the General Social Survey (GSS) Cumulative File 1972-2012. It consists of 57061 observations with 114 variables. Each variable corresponds to a specific question asked to the respondent. Questions cover a diverse range of issues including national spending priorities, marijuana use, crime and punishment, race relations, quality of life, confidence in institutions, and sexual behavior. The data could project biases as it pertains a long list of questions and the respodents willingness to answer them all.

the study is generalizabile to the US general population, however causal assumptions are not not taken into account due to random sampling and random assignment of treatment was not done. thus any conclusive statement are based on correlation or association based inference.

Part 2: Research question

There seems a lot of polarization between the different point of views on societal discussions like healthcare and environment. adding on, someones opinion are often cornered into a politcal side e.g.pro-climate is democrat and more conservative views of climate change is occupied by the republicans. However, is the latter actually true? The purpose of the analysis is too cut through narrative and assumptions and look at it in a statistical way.

Has political affilation impact on someones attitute towards improving and protecting the environment?

The two categorical variables that will be used are :

  • partyid : Politcal party affilation
  • natenvir : Attitude towards improving and protecting environment.

Part 4: Inference

#####Hypothesis H0: Party affilation and attitude towards protection and improvement of environment are indepedent.

Ha: Party affilation and attitude towards protection and improvement of environment are depedent.

#####Method The two variables of interest are categorical variables -partyid and natenvir- and how they relate to each other, both variables consits of multiple levels. For that reason, a chi-square test of indepedence seems the most fitting.

#####Conditions for chi-square test

  1. Indepedence: sampled observations must be indepedent.
  2. Random sample assignment
  3. If sampling without replacement, n < 10 % of population
  4. Each case only contributes to one cell

The sample size is < 10 % of the US population and each result is counted in one cell.

  1. Sample size
  2. Each particular scenario (i.e.cell) must have atleast 5 expected cases.
    3. There are more than five expected cases for each scenario.

#####Inference test

Because the nature of the study -observational, we can only assume assocication and not causation between the variables.

chisq.test(df$partyid, df$natenvir)$expected

#####Interpretation

Pearsons Chi-squared test leads to a high X-squared with 14 degrees of freedom and a p-value of <2.2e-16. Hence, with an alpha of 0.05, we can reject the null-hypothesis in favor of the alternative hypothesis. concluding that there is evidence that one attitude towards spending in protecting end improving the environment is depedent on political affilation.
